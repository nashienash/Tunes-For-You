DID YOU KNOW?

Music might be one of the oldest forms of human expression! Long before words were invented, early humans were already surrounded by sounds from nature like birds singing, the wind blowing, and rain falling on leaves. These natural rhythms inspired them to clap, stomp, or use sticks and stones to create beats. Archaeologists have even discovered flutes made from bird bones that are over 40,000 years old. Some researchers believe that humans may have sung before they spoke, using music to share feelings, tell stories, or connect with others. In a way, music has been with us since the very beginning!
